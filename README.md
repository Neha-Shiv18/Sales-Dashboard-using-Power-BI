# Sales-Dashboard-using-Power-BI

#### Overview 🧾 

In an era where AI tools can generate dashboards instantly, this project focuses on building a sales dashboard from scratch using traditional data analytics techniques in Power BI.

The goal was to understand data modeling, DAX calculations, and visualization design rather than relying on automated tools.

In an era where AI tools can generate dashboards instantly, this project focuses on building a sales dashboard from scratch using traditional data analytics techniques in Power BI.

The goal was to understand data modeling, DAX calculations, and visualization design rather than relying on automated tools.

##### 🎯 Objectives
Analyze sales performance
Track Month-on-Month (MoM) growth
Identify top customers and segments
Understand category and gender-based trends


#### 📌 Key Features
KPI metrics: Total Sales, Profit, Profit %, MoM Growth, cost of goods - using DAX 
Customer segmentation (Low, Medium, High value)
Age groups segmentation 
Top 5 customers using DAX ranking
Interactive slicer- Year 
Dynamic visuals and filters

### 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
Power BI for visualizations 
DAX and Power Query for data cleaning and measuring more attirbutes. 

### Key insights 
The average order value (AOV) remained relatively stable, indicating minimal change in customer spending per order.
The majority of customers fall within the 40–59 age group, suggesting this segment drives a significant portion of sales activity.
In 2022, female customers contributed the highest sales across all categories. However, in 2023, male customers showed a notable increase in spending, particularly in the Electronics category.
A benchmark of 5% Month-on-Month (MoM) sales growth was set. The business exceeded expectations, achieving 15.07% growth in 2022 and a significantly higher 320.41% growth in 2023, indicating strong performance beyond targets.
Cost of production (COGS) has decreased over time, leading to a marginal improvement in profit margins.
